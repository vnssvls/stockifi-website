# Blueprint — Home

> Wireframe guide for DC-471. Section-by-section: purpose, the content blocks the section must hold, and the copy intent (not final copy). Doubles as the field list for the Astro content schema later.
> Page `/` · static · **Primary kw:** restaurant food cost software · **Secondary:** food cost management software, cost control for restaurants
> **Audience:** operator/owner/finance at an established restaurant or group, evaluating cost-control tools (commercial intent).
> **Goal / CTA:** Book a demo (→ `/contact`).
> **Angle:** see exactly where your margins leak, built for European operators. (Lead on outcome + EU fit. NOT "we automate invoices" — table stakes. NOT the guarantee — that's a closer.)

---

### 1. Hero — promise
- **Goal:** one sharp outcome promise + primary CTA, above the fold.
- **Blocks:** H1 (outcome) · 1-line subhead · primary CTA "Book a demo" · secondary link (e.g. "See how it works") · hero visual (product/dashboard or moody real photo).
- **Copy intent:** the margin-leak outcome for EU operators. Plain, not clever.
- **Source:** sharpen current hero ("Cut Your Hospitality Costs. Without the Manual Work."). Clarity bar = MarginEdge.

### 2. Social proof bar — credibility
- **Goal:** instant trust right after the promise.
- **Blocks:** "Trusted by…" line · client logos (SkiGeilo, Eik&Friends, Schnitzelei, etc.) · a hard number (500+ restaurants) · rating if we have one.
- **Source:** current site logo row + proof points.

### 3. How it works — mechanism (overview)
- **Goal:** show the 4-step model fast, so they get *how* it works.
- **Blocks:** 4 steps (Recipes → Accounting/Invoices → POS → Inventory & Variance), each = icon + label + one line. Link to product pages.
- **Copy intent:** recipes + ingredients first, then integration (per the how-it-works standard).

### 4. Product pillars — capabilities
- **Goal:** route to the 4 product pages.
- **Blocks:** 4 cards: Recipe Costing · Supplier Price Tracking · POS & Consumption · Inventory & Variance. Each = title + one line + link.

### 5. Outcomes / quantified results — proof
- **Goal:** metrics beat adjectives.
- **Blocks:** 1-3 hard numbers (e.g. 2-3% COGS improvement) · a testimonial quote with attribution · link to Customer Stories.
- **Source:** current testimonials; pull from customer-stories content.

### 6. Segments — self-select
- **Goal:** "this is for me."
- **Blocks:** grid of 4: Independent restaurants · Restaurant groups · Hotels with F&B · Bars. Each links to its Solutions page.

### 7. Integrations — ecosystem fit
- **Goal:** lead with breadth (a real strength).
- **Blocks:** "Connect 69+ integrations" · logo wall (POS + accounting) · link to /integrations.

### 8. Risk-reversal band — the guarantee (small, near CTA)
- **Goal:** de-risk the decision *late*, not lead with it.
- **Blocks:** short line on the €3,000 / 30-day risk-free onboarding guarantee · link to Pricing. Keep it a slim band, not a hero. Frame as confidence, not "money back".

### 9. FAQ — objection handling
- **Goal:** close common B2B doubts before the demo.
- **Blocks:** 5-6 Q&A (integrations, setup time, pricing range, fit, data/language). Source: current FAQ + competitor FAQs.

### 10. Final CTA — convert
- **Goal:** restate the promise + push to demo.
- **Blocks:** headline · primary CTA "Book a demo" (→ /contact).

### 11. Footer
- **Blocks:** nav columns (Product, Solutions, Resources, Company), language switcher, legal, contact.
