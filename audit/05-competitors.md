# Competitive Landscape — Stockifi

> Workstream B (research input for SEO comparison pages + positioning). Direct competitors identified from Stockifi's actual product: recipe costing, automated invoice processing, accounting + POS integration, supplier price tracking, periodic inventory with variance.
> Not anchored to the three screenshot competitors. Researched 2026-05-30.

## What Stockifi is (positioning anchor)
Food-cost platform: live recipe costing, automated invoice processing via **accounting integration**, POS-driven consumption, periodic stock count with variance. **Integrates with POS and accounting, does not replace them.** No labour / payroll / scheduling. EU/Nordic focus (NO, SE, DK, DE, NL, FR, UK, BE, AT, CH, HR).

## Positioning takeaways
1. **Closest threats on the differentiator:** MarginEdge, xtraCHEF, MarketMan, Apicbase. All automate invoices to update recipe costs. Stockifi's copy must say *why it does this better/differently for EU operators* (local accounting + POS integrations, supplier price tracking depth, language/market fit).
2. **Edge vs US players:** native EU/Nordic accounting + POS integrations, local presence, multi-language. The US incumbents serve EU "generally" but are not built for it.
3. **Edge vs Apicbase (EU peer):** automation-first and food-cost-focused, not a sprawling full-F&B suite.
4. **Deliberate non-overlap:** competitors increasingly bundle labour/payroll/scheduling (Nory, R365, CrunchTime). Stockifi stays focused on food cost. Frame as focus, not a gap.
5. **Does not replace accounting** (unlike Restaurant365) — a clarity point, not a weakness.

## Comparison-page shortlist (recommend to Simon)
Safe + high-value: **Apicbase, MarketMan, MarginEdge, WISK, Supy, FoodNotify** (FoodNotify especially for DE). Hold on **Munu / Lightspeed** (integration partners) and **xtraCHEF** (Toast-locked, niche). Every claim sourced from primary sites, balanced framing, Simon sign-off (per content-validation rule).
