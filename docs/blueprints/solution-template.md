# Blueprint — Solution page template

> One layout for all 8 Solutions pages, two content variants: by-segment and by-role.
> Segment: Independent restaurants · Restaurant groups · Hotels with F&B · Bars
> Role: Owners & GMs · Finance · Head Chefs · Operations
> Type: static · **Goal/CTA:** Book a demo (→ `/contact`).

## Per-page primary keyword (from keyword map)
Segment: food cost software for small restaurants · multi location restaurant inventory software · hotel F&B cost control software · bar inventory software.
Role: restaurant profitability software · restaurant cost control software · recipe cost management · restaurant operations cost control.

## The variant difference
- **Segment pages:** speak to the *operation type* (its scale, structure, typical pains).
- **Role pages:** speak to the *person's job-to-be-done* (what that role is accountable for, what Stockifi gives them).

## Sections
### 1. Hero — "Stockifi for [segment/role]"
- **Blocks:** H1 naming the audience + their outcome · subhead · CTA.
- **Copy intent:** make them feel seen ("this is for me").

### 2. Their specific pains
- **Blocks:** 2-3 pains unique to this segment/role (e.g. groups = recipe cost cascade across sites; finance = no visibility into actual vs theoretical).
- **Copy intent:** industry-accurate, no generic SaaS pain.

### 3. How Stockifi helps them
- **Blocks:** map the relevant capabilities to their pains (subset of the 4 pillars that matter most to this audience).

### 4. Proof for this audience
- **Blocks:** a client of this type (segment) or a role-relevant metric · link to a story.
- **Note:** groups → multi-location client; bars → a bar client, etc.

### 5. Features that matter most
- **Blocks:** 2-3 feature highlights with links to the relevant product pages.

### 6. CTA band
- **Blocks:** audience-specific restate + "Book a demo".

## Metadata (per page)
Title + description on the page's primary keyword.

## Internal links out
Relevant product pages · other solution pages · a fitting customer story · /pricing · /contact.

## Notes
- Reuse the same component layout for all 8; only copy + proof swap.
- Industry accuracy is critical here (see the never-get-wrong rules): groups share suppliers (problem = recipe cost cascade, not price variance across sites); weekly counting is uncommon in EU; hotels are a fit only with full F&B.
