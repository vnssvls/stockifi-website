# Product image prompts — film flash photography (Jun 2026)

Follows the SEO-thumbnail prompt structure (`feedback_thumbnail_editorial_direction.md`):
film stock + style → setting → subject posture → action → photographer position →
light → found detail → texture → identity hook → closing constraints.

Two deliberate departures from the thumbnail spec for these PRODUCT images:
1. **Tablets/phones are allowed** (showing the Stockifi app in use is the point).
2. **Direct on-camera flash on film** is the lead aesthetic per V's brief.

Closing line for all: `Aspect ratio 16:9, composed for a safe square crop.`

## 1 — Inventory transfers (kitchen, chef in the app, staff mid-prep behind)
Shot in the style of observational contemporary documentary photography with direct on-camera flash on Cinestill 800T, hard flash falloff into shadow, mild halation around highlights, fine film grain, cold flash mixing with warm tungsten ambient. Mid-prep in a working restaurant kitchen, early evening before service. A head chef in a dark apron stands at the stainless pass holding a tablet at chest height, thumb mid-tap on the screen, head tilted down in the focused half-second of logging something, face in three-quarter profile and never toward the lens. Behind, two prep cooks are caught mid-task in soft motion, one lifting a hotel pan, the other leaning into a cutting board, both blurred by shallow focus. The photographer stands a few steps back at the end of the line, hip height, shooting past the stainless edge of the counter that clips the lower-left foreground. The direct flash lands hard on the chef's forearm and the tablet edge while the kitchen beyond falls into warm pendant glow and shadow. Faint apron embroidery at the chest. Mild film grain, a thin flash shadow thrown to the right, warm tones, 35mm. Shallow depth of field with the chef's hands and the tablet in sharp focus. No readable text anywhere on the screen, signage, or labels. No-one facing the camera. Aspect ratio 16:9, composed for a safe square crop.
